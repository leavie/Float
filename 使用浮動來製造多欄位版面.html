The Evolution of Bicycle

The First Bicycle

In 1817 Baron von Drais invented a walking machine that would help him get around the royal gardnes faster.

The device know as the Draisienne (or "hobby horse") was made of wood, and propelled by pushing your feed on the ground in gilding movement.

It was not seen a suitable for any place other than a well maintained pathway.

Further Innovations

In 1865, the velocipede (missing "fast foot") attached pedals to the front wheel, but its wooden structure mad it extremely uncomfortable.

In 1870 the first all-metal machine appeared. The pedals were attached directly to the front wheel.

Solid rubber tired and the long spokes of the large front wheel provided a much smoother ride than is predecessor.
